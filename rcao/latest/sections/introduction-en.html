Introduction back to ToC

The Resilient Collective Awareness Ontology (RCAO) is an OWL2 ontology designed to model the domain of collective awareness in automated driving. It provides a structured vocabulary for representing the dynamic states of awareness that emerge from the interaction of various traffic participants, including automated driving systems, human drivers, and intelligent infrastructure. RCAO extends the W3C PROV-O Ontology to describe the provenance of awareness states, linking them to the agents, activities, and entities involved in their generation. By aligning with established ontologies like AGO (Automotive Ontology), RCAO facilitates interoperability and the integration of data from diverse sources within the automotive ecosystem. The primary goals of RCAO are to: 1. Provide a formal model for different levels of awareness, from basic perception to comprehension and future projection. 2. Enable the explicit representation of resilience and trust attributes associated with awareness information. 3. Support the traceability of awareness states by capturing their derivation from various information sources, including sensors and V2X messages. 4. Facilitate the development of more robust and resilient collective awareness systems for connected and automated vehicles. This document specifies the classes and properties of the RCAO ontology.
